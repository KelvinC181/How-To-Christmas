Roast Turkey

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (4-6 kg)
  • 150g butter, softened
  • 2 lemons, halved
  • 1 bulb garlic, halved horizontally
  • 2 onions, quartered
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 500ml chicken stock (for basting)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Remove giblets from the turkey. Pat the turkey dry and season the cavity with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff the cavity with lemons, garlic, onions, rosemary, and thyme.
  4. Loosen the skin of the turkey breast by gently sliding your hand under it, and spread softened butter between the skin and the meat.
  5. Rub remaining butter over the skin of the turkey. Season the skin with salt and pepper.
  6. Place turkey in a roasting pan, breast-side up. Pour the chicken stock into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast for approximately 13-15 minutes per pound, basting every 30 minutes with pan juices. Cover loosely with foil if the skin starts browning too much.
  8. Check for doneness with a meat thermometer (internal temperature should be 74°C/165°F in the thickest part of the breast).
  9. Let the turkey rest, covered with foil, for 30 minutes before carving.

Yorkshire Pudding

Ingredients

  • 200g plain flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 200ml milk
  • 100ml water
  • Vegetable oil or beef drippings

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).
  2. Whisk the flour, eggs, milk, and water together until smooth and lump-free. Rest the batter for 30 minutes.
  3. Pour a small amount of oil or drippings into each hole of a 12-hole muffin tin. Place the tin in the oven to heat until the oil is smoking hot.
  4. Quickly pour the batter into the tin, filling each hole about two-thirds full.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes without opening the oven door, until the puddings are puffed and golden.

Christmas Pudding

Ingredients

  • 200g suet or butter, grated
  • 200g breadcrumbs
  • 250g brown sugar
  • 200g plain flour
  • 250g dried mixed fruits
  • 100g candied peel
  • 2 apples, grated
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 3 large eggs
  • 150ml dark beer (e.g., stout)
  • 50ml brandy (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix the suet, breadcrumbs, sugar, flour, fruits, peel, spices, and apple in a large bowl.
  2. Stir in the orange zest, juice, eggs, beer, and brandy. Mix until combined and thick.
  3. Grease a pudding basin and spoon in the mixture, pressing down lightly. Cover with parchment paper, then foil, and tie securely with string.
  4. Steam for 6 hours, topping up the water as needed.
  5. Cool and store in a cool, dry place. Steam again for 2 hours before serving.

Treacle Sponge

Ingredients

  • 150g golden syrup
  • 150g self-raising flour
  • 150g butter, softened
  • 150g sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp milk

Instructions

  1. Grease a pudding basin and pour the golden syrup into the bottom.
  2. Beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually add the eggs, one at a time, beating well.
  3. Fold in the flour and vanilla extract, then stir in the milk.
  4. Spoon the mixture over the syrup and cover the basin with parchment paper and foil.
  5. Steam for 2 hours, ensuring the water is topped up as needed.
  6. Turn out onto a plate and serve with custard.

Apple Crumble

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 6 apples (e.g., Bramley), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 50g sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

For the crumble topping:

  • 200g plain flour
  • 100g butter, chilled and cubed
  • 100g sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Toss the apples with sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Spread in a baking dish.
  3. Rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Stir in the sugar.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes until the topping is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Serve warm with custard, cream, or ice cream.

Beef Wellington

Ingredients

  • 1kg beef tenderloin (center-cut)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 250g mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 100g pâté (optional)
  • 8 slices prosciutto
  • 500g puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper, then sear in a hot pan with olive oil for 2-3 minutes on each side until browned. Let it cool.
  3. In the same pan, cook the chopped mushrooms over medium heat until all the moisture evaporates and they become dry. Let the mixture cool.
  4. Spread pâté (if using) over the cooled beef, then top with the mushroom mixture.
  5. Lay out the prosciutto slices on a sheet of plastic wrap, then place the beef on top and roll it tightly. Chill in the fridge for 10 minutes.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface and wrap the beef in it. Seal the edges and brush with the beaten egg.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown and the internal temperature of the beef reaches 50-55°C (120-130°F) for rare, or 60°C (140°F) for medium.
  8. Rest the Wellington for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Lemon Meringue Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 200g sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 480ml water
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 60g butter
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for meringue)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a saucepan, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add the water and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until thickened.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks, then slowly temper with the hot sugar mixture. Return everything to the saucepan and cook for 2-3 more minutes. Remove from heat and stir in butter, lemon juice, and zest.
  4. Pour the lemon filling into the pre-baked pie crust.
  5. In a clean bowl, beat the egg whites with cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add 1/4 cup sugar and continue to beat until stiff peaks form.
  6. Spread the meringue over the lemon filling, sealing the edges. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown.
  7. Let the pie cool before serving.

Chocolate Lava Cake

Ingredients

  • 150g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 115g butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 50g sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 30g plain flour
  • Butter and cocoa powder (for greasing ramekins)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease four ramekins with butter and dust with cocoa powder.
  2. In a heatproof bowl, melt the chocolate and butter together over simmering water, stirring until smooth.
  3. Whisk the eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined. Gradually add the melted chocolate mixture and stir in the flour until smooth.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared ramekins, filling each about three-quarters full.
  5. Bake for 12-14 minutes until the edges are set but the center is still soft.
  6. Let the cakes rest for 1 minute before inverting onto plates. Serve immediately with ice cream or whipped cream.

Mince Pies

Ingredients

  • 225g cold butter, diced
  • 350g plain flour
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 300g mincemeat
  • Icing sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Make the pastry: Rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Stir in the sugar, then bind the mixture with the egg. Knead lightly, wrap in cling film, and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
  3. Roll out the pastry to about 3mm thick. Cut out circles with a pastry cutter and line a muffin tin with the circles.
  4. Spoon a teaspoon of mincemeat into each pastry case.
  5. Cut smaller circles or shapes for the lids. Place them on top and press edges to seal.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes until golden. Cool, dust with icing sugar, and serve.

Eggnog

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 500ml whole milk
  • 250ml double cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg (plus extra for garnish)
  • 120ml dark rum, bourbon, or brandy (optional)
  • 4 large egg whites (optional, for extra froth)

Instructions

  1. Whisk the egg yolks and sugar in a bowl until pale and thick.
  2. Heat the milk, cream, vanilla, and nutmeg in a saucepan over medium heat until steaming (do not boil).
  3. Gradually whisk the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook gently, stirring constantly, until it thickens slightly.
  4. Remove from heat and cool. Stir in the alcohol if using.
  5. If you want a frothy finish, whisk the egg whites until soft peaks form, then fold into the eggnog before serving.
  6. Chill, garnish with nutmeg, and serve.

Mulled Wine

Ingredients

  • 750ml red wine (e.g., Merlot or Shiraz)
  • 150ml orange juice
  • 2 oranges, sliced
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 3 star anise
  • 75g brown sugar
  • 50ml brandy (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine the wine, orange juice, orange slices, cinnamon sticks, cloves, star anise, and sugar in a large saucepan.
  2. Heat gently over low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring to dissolve the sugar. Do not let it boil.
  3. Stir in the brandy, if using, and remove from heat.
  4. Strain into mugs or glasses and garnish with orange slices or a cinnamon stick.
