Christmas Recipes

Share the taste of Christmas with your loved ones,
with the help of these classic festive recipes.

Roast Turkey

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (4-6 kg)
  • 150g butter, softened
  • 2 lemons, halved
  • 1 bulb garlic, halved horizontally
  • 2 onions, quartered
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 500ml chicken stock (for basting)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Remove giblets from the turkey. Pat the turkey dry and season the cavity with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff the cavity with lemons, garlic, onions, rosemary, and thyme.
  4. Loosen the skin of the turkey breast by gently sliding your hand under it, and spread softened butter between the skin and the meat.
  5. Rub remaining butter over the skin of the turkey. Season the skin with salt and pepper.
  6. Place turkey in a roasting pan, breast-side up. Pour the chicken stock into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast for approximately 13-15 minutes per pound, basting every 30 minutes with pan juices. Cover loosely with foil if the skin starts browning too much.
  8. Check for doneness with a meat thermometer (internal temperature should be 74°C/165°F in the thickest part of the breast).
  9. Let the turkey rest, covered with foil, for 30 minutes before carving.

Yorkshire Pudding

Ingredients

  • 200g plain flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 200ml milk
  • 100ml water
  • Vegetable oil or beef drippings

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).
  2. Whisk the flour, eggs, milk, and water together until smooth and lump-free. Rest the batter for 30 minutes.
  3. Pour a small amount of oil or drippings into each hole of a 12-hole muffin tin. Place the tin in the oven to heat until the oil is smoking hot.
  4. Quickly pour the batter into the tin, filling each hole about two-thirds full.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes without opening the oven door, until the puddings are puffed and golden.

Christmas Pudding

Ingredients

  • 200g suet or butter, grated
  • 200g breadcrumbs
  • 250g brown sugar
  • 200g plain flour
  • 250g dried mixed fruits
  • 100g candied peel
  • 2 apples, grated
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 3 large eggs
  • 150ml dark beer (e.g., stout)
  • 50ml brandy (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix the suet, breadcrumbs, sugar, flour, fruits, peel, spices, and apple in a large bowl.
  2. Stir in the orange zest, juice, eggs, beer, and brandy. Mix until combined and thick.
  3. Grease a pudding basin and spoon in the mixture, pressing down lightly. Cover with parchment paper, then foil, and tie securely with string.
  4. Steam for 6 hours, topping up the water as needed.
  5. Cool and store in a cool, dry place. Steam again for 2 hours before serving.

Treacle Sponge

Ingredients

  • 150g golden syrup
  • 150g self-raising flour
  • 150g butter, softened
  • 150g sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp milk

Instructions

  1. Grease a pudding basin and pour the golden syrup into the bottom.
  2. Beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually add the eggs, one at a time, beating well.
  3. Fold in the flour and vanilla extract, then stir in the milk.
  4. Spoon the mixture over the syrup and cover the basin with parchment paper and foil.
  5. Steam for 2 hours, ensuring the water is topped up as needed.
  6. Turn out onto a plate and serve with custard.

Apple Crumble

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 6 apples (e.g., Bramley), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 50g sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

For the crumble topping:

  • 200g plain flour
  • 100g butter, chilled and cubed
  • 100g sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Toss the apples with sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Spread in a baking dish.
  3. Rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Stir in the sugar.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes until the topping is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Serve warm with custard, cream, or ice cream.
